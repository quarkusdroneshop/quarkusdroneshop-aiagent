Executive Summary
===============================================================================

1. Introduction
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly transforming enterprise software.

However, many AI solutions today are implemented as isolated chatbots or
standalone applications that tightly couple prompts, business logic,
enterprise data, and system integrations.

Such architectures become difficult to maintain, difficult to govern,
and difficult to extend across enterprise systems.

Enterprise organizations require a different approach.

AI must become another platform capability rather than another application.

This document defines a reference architecture for building
Enterprise AI Agent Platforms on Red Hat OpenShift AI.

The architecture separates AI reasoning from business logic,
metadata management, and enterprise integration,
allowing organizations to safely introduce AI into existing systems.

The goal is not to build a chatbot.

The goal is to build an extensible enterprise platform
that enables reusable AI Agents across multiple business domains.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Vision
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The platform enables organizations to create reusable AI Agents that
can interact with enterprise services through standardized Tool Calling.

Each Agent focuses on reasoning.

Business services remain responsible for business logic.

Metadata remains managed by OpenMetadata.

Deployment remains cloud-native through OpenShift.

The platform must support future AI Agents without changing
the overall architecture.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. Design Goals
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The reference architecture pursues the following goals.

• AI Native

Artificial Intelligence is treated as a first-class platform capability.

• Cloud Native

Every component is designed for Kubernetes and OpenShift.

• Enterprise Ready

Security, governance, observability and scalability
are built into the platform.

• Vendor Neutral

The architecture avoids unnecessary vendor lock-in.

Open standards are preferred whenever possible.

• Metadata Driven

Metadata is externalized and managed by OpenMetadata.

AI never owns business metadata.

• Tool First

Every enterprise capability is exposed through Tools.

Agents never communicate directly with enterprise systems.

• API First

Every business capability is exposed as REST APIs.

Business APIs remain independent from AI.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Core Principles
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 1

AI performs reasoning.

AI never executes business logic.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 2

Business APIs own business logic.

Business rules are never embedded inside prompts.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 3

Metadata belongs to OpenMetadata.

Metadata is the single source of truth.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 4

Every external capability becomes a Tool.

Agents interact only with Tools.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 5

Agents are stateless.

Conversation state is externalized.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 6

Business services remain independently deployable.

AI shall never require changes to existing services.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principle 7

OpenShift provides the runtime platform.

Every component is designed for OpenShift.

9. Summary
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Enterprise AI is not another application.

Enterprise AI is a platform.

By separating AI reasoning,
business logic,
metadata,
and enterprise services,

organizations can safely adopt AI
without sacrificing maintainability,
governance,
or scalability.

This reference architecture provides
a practical blueprint
for building production-grade
Enterprise AI Agent Platforms
on Red Hat OpenShift AI.
